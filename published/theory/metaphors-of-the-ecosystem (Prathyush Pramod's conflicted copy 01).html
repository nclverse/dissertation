2.1 Metaphors of the Ecosystem

This dissertation has been in major parts been one of building two different worlds: The user facing interface of the system and the underlying world of computations that generate the visual artefacts. The nature of these two systems I have been able to realize are vastly different. The principles form a blueprint out of which I have crafted the software for the ecosystem.

The Two worlds

The user interface design of a software is utmost critical to the efficienct functioning of an application.

[Reference about face here]

While design side of things allows for infinite imagination it is constrained by the underlying world of processes constraints it because of the limits of the computation.

This is a visualization of how I model the two systems: The upper layer being the front end user facing side of the underlying structures. The dissertation will talk about how the ideas and concepts of the first world are more cogent than the logic layer that lays hidden beneath. It can also be reflective of where my strengths and weaknesses lay.

[Insert picture here]

The Visual World

The first world is that of the user facing end of the software. This comprises of everything visual as well as the not so visual (UX) part of the system. Even though parameters such as responsiveness plays a role in determining how the user experience is impacted, for the large part the effects are visual. When it comes to the visual user interface, the possibilities of representations are almost only limited by the imagination of the designer. Anything that is the whim of the designer can be conjured up and represented on the screen but the key factors that played pivotal role in this project where in developing a visual language. Since this project attempts to create a suite of apps directed towards student learning, it was of utmost importance that two factors were given foremost importance. Primarily, it had to be ensured that no time of the user was wasted and secondly that there was a coherent narrative spread through the app and how it is all connected together. I made made use of some metaphors to ensure this tight integration and it will be detailed in the following section. But now to detail about the second kind of world.

The Computation World

These systems are built on a substrate of complex underlying systems where things are very fragile if you are not careful. This is because the underground is always changing and could potentially lead to breakdown of whole software ecosystem if they are ever allowed to unmonitored changes. Though the software system presents many ways to organize this complexity, the basic nature of breaking on update is still incumbent/popular. [Invoke analogy with garden and roots that run the system. Cut the roots and the garden disappears]. This is because the frameworks are ever changing owing to the whims of the makers. One way is to start from scratch and supply as much care and control over everything by building them from the ground up but the scope of this dissertation as it had to be produced in a limited time didn't allow for such levels of explorations. With a potential to step on each other's toes at least as often as they stand on each other's shoulders.

Keyword: Interoperability between technologies.

Many languages will be spoken by the system and hence the central database for communication is named Ivory Tower which is the name of the tower of Babel which had a unified language. So the tower of babel provides the source material which the programming languages then use to describe the processes. It is to be noted that even though the current systems only speak in one language, Clojure, it is architected in a way it allows for polyglot and diverse styles of programming.

The degree to which all the disparate pieces can work together. We think in terms of metaphors, and the metaphors we use influence how we think. Reference Metaphors We Live By. Hence it was key to adopt an overarching metaphor for both of these worlds.

On juggling mental models

As software construction goes it requires the frameworks to be internally consistent and follow some contracts. Much like how a function deals with an input and chains it on to another. But when a developer starts working with this, he is faced with the challenge of keeping everything intact.

The gestalt of the system

The systems that I am about to describe can be understood in terms of metaphors relating to other systems. What I have envisioned is to create a system with a central core, as the term gestalt suggests is to create something that is more fundamental than the sum of it's parts. The way I hope to achieve it is by creating a system which is seamlessly navigatable and employes a common lexicon of terms to describe the various concepts that occur throughout the system.

Metaphors employed here are primarily deviced as to give a narrative structure and give a fully coherent meaning towards what I was trying to achieve in the end. It would be able to infer from these propositions how my actions were directed towards certain ends and whenever certain things were made to be consistent in a particular way, I'll be invoking these metaphors to explain why certain tradeoffs had to be made for consistency.

Building an Experential Gestalt

In their book, Metaphors We Live By, George Lakoff and Mark Johnson talk of something known as experential gestalt. They contend that a cluster of other components are experienced as a whole by human beings which they find more basic than the parts. My aim is to give the ecosystem that I'm building a feel with all the individual apps collaborating in a seamless manner to form a coherent whole. This directly references the work of a prototype done by Rosch in her work on categorizations done by humans. A recurrent complex of properties, our concept of causation is at once holistic, analyzable into those properties and capable of a wide range of variation. So one app in the ecosystem can be seen as a prototype.
