2.4 Nature of Software Development

Almost all of this project was completed in a programming language known as Clojure. It's a lisp that runs on the JVM. As of the writing it's only getting a popular mindshare but it has a solid community, great tooling. The reason I opted for Clojure was that it looked like a fun language. But this have been transformative in more ways than one. Rich abstractions. Immutability. Referential transparency and the [] they give for these concepts have greatly shaped the way I program.

Wavefronts of change

Software that is created in the system will be repeatedly hit by wavefronts of changes initiated by more than one agent at more than one location. It is of utmost importance that the philosophy and the principles laid out won't stand absolute towards these. These are formed at a time when constructing the system was the priority#1 and this is to be evolved as things change in the future.

Developing the Architecture

Both the process and the product of planning, designing and constructing buildings. Develop a language as you describe systems based on this architecture. This makes the architecture tangible and provides an unambiguous description of architectural building blocks as well as concrete system while staying away from technology decisions.

The architecture requires definition of the building blocks (types of things) from which the actual system will be bulit. It can be thought of as a language to talk about the system.

Giving something a name

Most interesting systems are complex because of the simple fact that there are a lot of entities and myriads of relationships among them. The only way we can hope to bring order to this chaos is by spotting patterns that helps us manipulate and reason about these concepts. As someone said: Giving a thing a name gives you control over it.

Holistic vs. Reductionistic Approach

I have increasingly noticed that bespoke constructs and structures and performance optimizations most of the time leads to compromise in the generality of the software. This is not the main focal points of this dissertation. This is not to mean that they are de-emphasized but because of the nature of the project where the end users requirements are not available prior to development, it is most sensible to keep them flexible and build composable abstractions that are as general as possible and helps to solve a wide range of problems. Focusing or improving upon a certain facet of a software means that if that component gets un-used in the end, all the effort goes waste.
