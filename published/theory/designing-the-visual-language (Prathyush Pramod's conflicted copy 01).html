2.3 Designing the Visual Language

One of the main challenges towards evolving a system is developing it's visual language. Inorder to create a harmonious user interface that tells a coherent narrative to the user, it has to be ensured that harmony is to be maintained across the whole spectrum of visual representation ranging from a coherent visual language at the macro level down to the microlevel maintaining consistency in the knowledge representative artifacts such as icon labels and module codes and color codes. Discrepancy among any of the variables spread across this spectrum would result in non optimal results in the end product.

Coherence over Consistency

The design methodology adopted for this project eschews using the same visual design language as proposed by the style guides [link] [link] that are used these days. I have found from my experience over the years that this hinders the creativity of a designer. Since what is being created here is a platform I had to ensure that the future is kept in mind and all that this platform sets out is to have overall coherence. That is concurrency outweighs coherence.

Where possible consistency has been brought in, but I felt it's better to leave the consistency bit untouched because it requires a mature understanding of the ecosystem after it is unrolled and the user behaviours for the observation for about 6 months inorder to see what's working and what's not. Only a preliminary understanding of this has been achieved so far and it was most sensible to leave it open. This allows new design languages to be incorporated into the system at a later stage.

Consistency leads to shoehorning the concepts whereas coherence gives an overall picture. A system was to use a cogent visual language that covered the whole spectrum of the apps. A lot of principles in constructing the systems were borrowed from Design of Everyday Things and the Tufte tetrology. I greatly believe in the concept of personal computing as an amplifier for human reach. And most if not all of the concepts governing the UI has starts flowing having this as the central precept. Inorder to maintain this, the things that had to be done were tight feedback loop. Conceptual Integrity was maintained. Designing with the user in mind.

Each app has a primary colour and a secondary colour for highlighting. The behaviour is made consistent with the help of user interface patterns like the navigation bar on the top.

Design Heuristics

Caution has to be administered One other thing that I have followed throughout is to minimize the requirement of user interaction with the elements on screen. Even thought two clicks might only amount to 0.5 seconds. This two seconds when conducted by 4000 users doing it 10 times means that it's 4000 * 2 * 0.5 = 5 human hours on a big scale lost due to a dubious mistake from our side. Hence, we have supplied much of our attention in showing the data and hiding away the irrelevant. This is not entirely perfect yet, but we feel I have strove to adhere to this principle wherever possible.

The corollary of this is that actions that which are not used frequently are hidden behind two level actions.

The design philosophy

There schools of thought in user interface design was recently bifurcated with the advent of so called flat design. Both comes with a set of guiding principles and the philosophy I follow here is a synthesis of ideas from both worlds. I have tried to unify the shaded look that is characteristic of the skeumorphic school and the flat design school and appropriate them to the right context.
